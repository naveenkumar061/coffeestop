French Press Coffee

Something about drinking coffee from a cylindrical pot with a stylish plunger makes you think of leisurely, romantic mornings when drinking coffee is a luxury rather than something you need to wake you up. The French press method only involves squeezing hot water through freshly ground coffee to create a flavorful drink that is best enjoyed in bed.

Espresso Coffee

An espresso serves as the foundation for the majority of Western variations and is the most popular coffee order in Italy. If you're feeling brave, approach a bar—what they call cafes in the European nation—and order a caffe or an espresso Romano. The latter has a thin layer of lemon peel added for tang and a spoonful of sugar for sweetness.

Details

Irish Coffee

Given that it contains both alcohol and caffeine for a double-kick, it is understandable why the Irish coffee is arguably the naughtiest variant on this list. A generous shot of Irish whiskey, a spoonful of brown sugar, and thick heavy cream are all added to the rich, fragrant beverage. The original recipe calls for non-whipped versions.
